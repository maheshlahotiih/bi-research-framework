# Business Intelligence Research Framework

A structured approach to conducting lawful competitive intelligence, vendor assessment,  
and due diligence using publicly available information.

## Overview

This framework provides a comprehensive methodology for turning public business  
information into actionable intelligence through multi-source verification, entity  
disambiguation, temporal analysis, and confidence scoring.

## Core Capabilities

- Multi-source verification with tiered source reliability
- Entity disambiguation using multiple identifiers
- Temporal analysis with freshness checks
- Contradiction detection and resolution
- Gap analysis and limitation tracking
- Confidence scoring for all findings
- Clear source attribution and methodology documentation

## Use Cases

- **Vendor Assessment:** Evaluate potential suppliers and partners
- **M&A Research:** Due diligence for mergers and acquisitions
- **Competitive Intelligence:** Track competitor activities and market positioning
- **Market Intelligence:** Industry analysis and trend identification
- **Risk Assessment:** Identify business continuity and regulatory concerns

## Safety & Compliance

This framework operates strictly within legal and ethical boundaries:

- Uses ONLY publicly available, legally accessible sources
- Respects privacy regulations (GDPR/CCPA)
- Focuses on business-relevant information
- Respects rate limits and terms of service
- No unauthorized access or exploitation
- No circumventing access controls
- No stalking, harassment, or inappropriate surveillance

## Research Focus Areas

### A) Company Profile & Footprint

- Legal entity structure, brands, subsidiaries
- Leadership team (senior executives, business context)
- Product/service portfolio
- Entity disambiguation

### B) Financial & Corporate Health

- Public filings and financial reports
- Funding rounds and acquisitions
- Regulatory filings and compliance
- Credit ratings and analyst assessments

### C) Reputation & Market Position

- News coverage from credible outlets
- Industry analyst reports
- Market share and competitive positioning
- Aggregated customer/employee sentiment

### D) Legal & Regulatory

- Lawsuits and settlements (public records)
- Regulatory actions and compliance issues
- Intellectual property matters
- Material contracts (when publicly disclosed)

### E) Supply Chain & Partnerships

- Public vendor relationships
- Strategic partnerships and alliances
- Known dependencies

### F) Risk Indicators

- Business continuity concerns
- Management changes
- Restructuring or workforce changes
- Public incident disclosures

## Source Reliability Framework

### Tier 1 (High Confidence - Weight 1.0)

- Government records, regulatory filings, court documents
- Official company disclosures (10-K, 10-Q, press releases)
- Primary source documents

### Tier 2 (Medium-High Confidence - Weight 0.7)

- Major news outlets (Reuters, WSJ, Bloomberg, Financial Times)
- Established analyst firms (Gartner, Forrester, S&P)
- Verified professional profiles

### Tier 3 (Medium Confidence - Weight 0.4)

- Industry publications and trade journals
- Specialized business blogs with track records
- Conference presentations and whitepapers

### Tier 4 (Low Confidence - Weight 0.1)

- Single-source claims
- Anonymous reports
- Unverified social media (clearly marked)

## Evidence Standards

| Confidence Level | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Critical Claims | 1× Tier 1 OR 2× Tier 2 sources |
| High Confidence | 2× Tier 2 OR 1× Tier 2 + 2× Tier 3 |
| Medium Confidence | 3× Tier 3 |
| Low Confidence | Mark as “Unverified—requires additional validation” |

## Output Structure

### Level 1: Executive Summary

- Overall assessment
- Key findings (top 3-5)
- Critical attention areas
- Primary recommendations

### Level 2: Detailed Analysis

For each category:

- Finding title and summary
- Supporting evidence with citations
- Source tier and date
- Confidence level
- Implications
- Recommended actions

### Level 3: Source Documentation

- Complete source list with dates
- Search methodology used
- Information excluded and why

### Level 4: Limitations & Gaps

- What could not be verified
- Information age and staleness concerns
- Areas requiring additional research
- Known limitations of analysis

## Workflow

1. Intake: Clarify scope and objectives
2. Planning: Design research approach
3. Collection: Gather evidence with source tracking
4. Validation: Verify entity identity and source credibility
5. Analysis: Check freshness, detect contradictions, identify gaps
6. Synthesis: Structure findings into actionable report
7. Recommendations: Provide prioritized next steps
